Building an AI-Powered Bakery Customer Assistant
AI Lab Case Study
ChatGPT Custom GPT STORM AI Chatbase
Introduction

I'm a graduate student in Indiana Wesleyan University's Machine Learning Fundamentals course. This artifact documents my work in the course's AI Lab, where I explored multiple generative AI tools and then applied a design-thinking process to plan, build, and test a functional AI chatbot.

Description

This artifact captures a multi-stage exploration of generative AI: comparing outputs across repeated LLM prompts, holding a multi-turn conversation with a custom GPT, evaluating a research-assistant tool (STORM AI) for source-backed writing, and finally planning and deploying a working AI chatbot for a bakery customer-service use case.

Objective

To build practical fluency with different categories of generative AI tools, and to apply a design-thinking framework (empathy, problem definition, ideation, prototyping, testing) to go from a concept to a deployed, working AI product.

Process

I began by running an identical structured prompt across three separate LLM chats to compare consistency and constraint-following. I then had an extended conversation with a custom GPT, deliberately working through six interaction types (requesting changes, adding context, asking for improvements, probing its reasoning, posing hypotheticals, and requesting a summary) to test how well it retained instructions. Next, I used STORM AI to generate a sourced research article, examining its self-directed research process and noting where it explicitly declined to answer due to insufficient sources rather than guessing. Finally, I answered ten design-thinking planning questions before building the assistant in Chatbase, reviewing its privacy policy and terms of service, and running five planned test scenarios against the deployed bot identifying and documenting where its actual behavior diverged from my plan.

Tools & Tech

ChatGPT (general LLM), a custom GPT (Master Chef Cooking, Food & Recipes, Nutrition, Diet), STORM AI (research assistant), Chatbase (no-code AI agent builder).

Value Proposition

Unique value: this artifact shows I can move beyond casually prompting an AI tool to systematically evaluating it checking its math, catching constraint violations, and noticing when its actual behavior deviates from a written plan, rather than assuming the first output is correct.

Relevance: for an evaluator assessing applied AI/ML competency, this demonstrates the full arc from conceptual planning to a deployed, tested artifact, along with the critical judgment to identify and reason about gaps rather than just report success.
